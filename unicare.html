Overview

Purpose & Goal

UniCare helps UCD students and staff book medical appointments, access mental health resources, and get practical nutrition guidance — without confusing systems or phone-call gymnastics.

Goal: Reduce friction, reduce emotional load, and make the next step obvious — especially for students already under stress.

Calm UX Stigma Reduction Booking Flow Accessibility Mental Health

The Problem

Getting help felt like
a part-time job

Booking was phone-based, time-limited, and slow. Mental health support carried stigma and hesitation. Nutrition guidance was hard to access and easy to ignore.

The existing system made getting help feel like a part-time job — which is ironic when you're already juggling actual classes, work, and trying to remember to eat vegetables.

Process

How I did it

1 — Discover → Insight

From surveys and student conversations, a clear pattern emerged: students weren't just short on time — they were often already stressed before they even started.

Key insight: When users feel vulnerable, tone and clarity matter as much as functionality. Students needed accessible, judgment-free support that fit their already chaotic lives.

2 — Define (Journeys + Emotional Moments)

I mapped the health journey across a typical week and identified three key emotional moments — Frustration with multiple calls, Hesitation around mental health stigma, and Relief when support is quick and clear.

3 — Design & Iterate

I explored multiple layouts and iterated toward clear navigation for three distinct needs (Appointments / Mental Health / Nutrition), a booking flow with fewer steps, and resource pages that feel supportive, not overwhelming.

Design Decisions

What I chose & why

Separated Pillars

Appointments, Mental Health, and Nutrition are clearly separated — reducing overwhelm and cognitive load.

Support-first Tone

Gentle, private language reduces stigma and hesitation around seeking mental health help.

Private Access Patterns

Self-help resources and gentle prompts lower the barrier to entry without forcing exposure.

Reduced Form Fields

Booking in 3 steps instead of 7 — because nobody wants to fill out their life story to see a doctor.

Final Interface

What I delivered

UniCare brings campus healthcare into one clear experience — Appointments to book GP/counseling with availability and confirmation, Mental Health guided resources with optional chat support, and Nutrition with practical meal guidance.

Outcome

Clarity is empathy

  • Faster access to care via a clearer booking flow
  • Lower barrier to mental health support through private, supportive design
  • More approachable nutrition guidance through simple, actionable content

The result: Students can actually focus on getting better instead of navigating bureaucracy.

What I Learned

Designing for sensitive experiences

Designing for sensitive experiences taught me that clarity is empathy. When users are already overwhelmed, the UI shouldn't add friction — it should quietly guide them forward.

Small decisions — reducing field inputs, choosing softer language, providing private access — made meaningful differences in how students felt about seeking help.

Next Steps

What comes next
